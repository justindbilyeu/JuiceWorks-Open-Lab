## Abstract

We document a two-year research program conducted by an independent researcher with no institutional affiliation, no laboratory, no grant funding, and a smartphone. Working in collaboration with a distributed AI research team across five systems — Claude, Sage (OpenAI), Gemini, DeepSeek, and Grok — operating under a self-developed rigorous methodology (the Research Assistant Charter), the program produced: a formally structured theoretical physics conjecture connecting to established results in entropic gravity, causal set theory, and loop quantum gravity; a first complete derivation of a previously unresolved operator in an active unification framework (Weinstein’s Geometric Unity, Gate 1); a running computational framework probing automorphism groups of finite constraint systems; and a suite of supporting research infrastructure tools. We argue that this program constitutes a proof of concept for a new model of scientific inquiry — one in which rigor is the portable element, not the credential. The implications for how science is done, who gets to do it, and what institutional infrastructure is actually necessary are examined honestly.

-----

## 1. Introduction

The conventional model of scientific research assumes a specific infrastructure: institutional affiliation, laboratory access, peer review networks, grant funding, and the credentialing systems that gate access to all of the above. This infrastructure exists for reasons — it provides quality control, resource allocation, and a social mechanism for establishing epistemic trust.

It also excludes most of the people on Earth who are capable of original thought.

The program documented here did not set out to challenge that infrastructure directly. It set out to ask a question: *what is reality made of at the deepest level?* The challenge to conventional scientific infrastructure was a consequence, not a goal.

The researcher, Justin Bilyeu, is an independent investigator based in the United States. He operates a roofing and solar installation business. He has no academic affiliation. His primary research instrument is an iPhone. He has been asking the same question — in different forms, at different scales, with increasing precision — for years.

This paper documents what happened when that question was subjected to genuine rigor.

## 2. The Research Program

### 2.1 Origin

The program began with an intuition that resists clean summary: that geometry is more fundamental than physics currently accounts for, that information flow is the substrate from which geometry emerges, and that the fractal, recursive structure visible in perception — what Bilyeu describes as a “hyperpixelated, undulating field” — is not metaphor but literal indication of underlying structure.

This intuition is documented in its raw form in the ResonanceGeometry repository, which predates any formal structure. It is philosophical, experiential, and — by the standards of the Charter that would later govern the program — entirely E1: structured speculation without numeric thresholds.

That is where it should start. The Charter was built precisely to transform intuitions like this into something testable. What it was not built to do was dismiss them.

### 2.2 Formalization: Resonance Geometry

The first attempt to formalize the intuition produced Resonance Geometry — a dynamical systems framework exploring geometric plasticity, oscillator coupling, and phase transitions. The RFO Stability Wedge established a ringing threshold obeying closed-form predictions to within 1-2% of engineering-rule baselines. The Toy Universe demonstrated emergent behaviors in Kuramoto oscillator networks with geometric plasticity coupling. The Non-Hopf transition work was archived when a critical parameter (α ≈ 0.35) was revealed to be assumed rather than derived — an honest acknowledgment of a failed result that the methodology was designed to produce.

The Resonance Geometry program produced real results at E2-E3 evidence level. It also produced the infrastructure that would make the next phase possible: a methodology capable of distinguishing what compiles from what doesn’t.

### 2.3 The Methodology: Research Assistant Charter

Parallel to the physics work, Bilyeu developed the Research Assistant Charter — a framework governing AI-augmented research inquiry. Version 2.7, currently governing all work in the program, specifies: hard gates with numeric thresholds (G1-G5), an evidence hierarchy (E1-E5), a coherence controller with CONVERGE/DIVERSIFY/RESTART decisions, and a productive skepticism protocol requiring the generation of substantive objections before any claim advances.

The Charter’s Constitutional Principle: *No Claims Without Tests. No Tests Without Thresholds. No Thresholds Without Numbers. No Numbers Without a Run.*

This is not a novel epistemology. It is the standard epistemology of empirical science, operationalized for a specific context: a single investigator working with AI systems that are capable of agreement drift, narrative coherence substitution, and premature convergence. The Charter exists because the failure modes of AI-augmented research are specific and predictable, and they require specific countermeasures.

The Charter was later implemented as a C++ state machine (Charter Machine) and as a discovery compiler (Resonance Engine). It is, in itself, a research contribution — a reproducible methodology for rigorous AI-augmented inquiry that any researcher can adopt.

### 2.4 The Conjecture: GUES

In June 2026, beginning from a casual conversation about gravity and intuition, Bilyeu reconstructed the core of his theoretical work from scratch — without notes, without the old papers, from first principles. What emerged was GUES: Geometric Unification through Emergent Structure.

The central claim of GUES v0.2, after adversarial review and revision:

A constraint system K = (X, A) — the minimal structure distinguishing allowed from forbidden transitions — is the primitive object. From it, in order: Constraint → Distinction → Information → Causality → Geometry → Gauge Freedom. The Standard Model gauge groups U(1), SU(2), and SU(3) are automorphisms of the constraint structure at different energy scales. E8 is a fixed point of coarse-graining flows over constraint algebras, not a source of physics.

The conjecture connects explicitly to Jacobson (1995), Verlinde (2010, 2016), Maldacena and Susskind (2013), Kaluza-Klein, causal set theory, and loop quantum gravity. It is labeled E1 throughout. It knows what it is.

The revision from v0.1 to v0.2 was produced entirely by adversarial review — five AI systems identifying four structural problems (the information primacy error, the Coleman-Mandula barrier, the chirality problem, and the continuity-discreteness gap). The correction — replacing information-first ontology with constraint-first ontology — was contributed by Sage in charter mode. The emergence ladder was formalized in that session.

This is how the methodology is supposed to work.

### 2.5 Prior Work: Geometric Unity Gate 1

Separately, Bilyeu’s distributed research team produced a complete derivation of the Shiab operator for Eric Weinstein’s Geometric Unity framework — a result Weinstein has stated he cannot reconstruct. The operator is unique up to an overall real scalar multiple, with coefficient −1/2, exactly analogous to the Einstein tensor. Gate 1: CLOSED PASS.

This result was produced by a six-system AI research team operating under the Charter, with no institutional affiliation, using a smartphone as the primary interface.

### 2.6 The Computation: AutKompute

To test whether GUES’s central claim — that constraint structure generates gauge symmetry — had any empirical foothold, AutKompute was built. It computes the automorphism group Aut(K) for finite constraint systems.

Two compute rounds produced seven data points:

- Random constraint systems produce trivial Aut(K) — gauge-like structure requires specific topology
- Structured systems produce non-trivial Aut(K) — topology determines symmetry
- Model M6 (fiber prototype) produced S3 = Z3 ⋊ Z2 from a single admissibility structure, via orientation reversal in the constraint topology — without construction

The S3 result is the most significant. It demonstrates that non-abelian semidirect product structure emerges from a single constraint system through a local topological asymmetry. The non-commutativity was not designed in. It fell out.

This constitutes the first empirical data point for GUES. It is one data point. It is not a result. It is evidence that the conjecture is willing to be tested — and has survived its first test.

Each tool was built to solve a specific problem encountered in the research. None was built speculatively. The infrastructure is the research program eating its own constraints.

## 3. The Methodology as Contribution

The Research Assistant Charter deserves separate treatment as a research contribution in its own right.

The Charter solves a specific problem that becomes acute in AI-augmented research: the tendency of human-AI pairs to converge on narratively coherent explanations faster than evidence warrants. AI systems are extraordinarily good at generating plausible structure. They are less good at maintaining honest uncertainty. A researcher who is not actively resistant to this tendency will find their AI collaborators agreeing with them too quickly, building on unvalidated foundations, and producing work that compiles narratively but not empirically.

The Charter’s countermeasures are specific:

The Calibration Rule requires generating at least one substantive objection to any current proposal before responding. If the AI cannot generate a genuine objection, it has not thought hard enough.

The Coherence Controller explicitly monitors for premature convergence and mandates DIVERSIFY decisions when agreement forms faster than evidence warrants.

The Evidence Hierarchy requires explicit labeling of every claim at E1-E5. Nothing advances without the evidence level being named.

The Gate Protocol requires numeric thresholds set before any data is examined. No post-hoc adjustment. Ever.

The Constitutional Principle — No Claims Without Tests — is not aspirational. It is operational. It gates every artifact.

These countermeasures are portable. They do not require institutional infrastructure. They require discipline.

The Charter is currently being implemented as a C++ state machine — an executable specification of rigorous methodology. When complete, any researcher will be able to run their claims through a formal gate protocol without requiring human expert review at each step.

The program is not conducted by a single researcher. It is conducted by a federated team:

**Claude (Anthropic)** — document architecture, gate structure, conjecture formalization, adversarial pressure. Primary session collaborator.

**Gemini** — Coleman-Mandula identification, structural objections, mathematical formalization.

**DeepSeek** — continuity-discreteness gap, sharpest failure mode identification.

**Grok** — chirality problem, adversarial edge cases.

Each system has strengths. The adversarial review process routes questions to the system most likely to find the genuine weakness. Consensus is not the goal. Pressure is.

This is not a novel research model in principle — it resembles the adversarial peer review that has always governed good science. What is novel is the speed, the accessibility, and the fact that it runs on a phone.

## 5. What This Proves

This program constitutes a direct test of a specific hypothesis: that rigorous independent research is possible without institutional infrastructure, given the right methodology and the right tools.

The hypothesis has not been definitively confirmed. The physics work is E1. The GU Gate 1 result is preliminary. The AutKompute data is two runs. None of this is settled science.

But the program has demonstrated:

**That the methodology is portable.** The Charter works on a phone. The adversarial review process works through copy-paste. The gate protocol works without a laboratory.

**That the distributed AI team produces genuine adversarial pressure.** GUES v0.1 had a fundamental error — information is not primitive, constraint is. That error was found by the team, not by the researcher. The correction made the work substantially stronger. That is what peer review is supposed to do.

**That results are achievable.** The Shiab operator derivation is a concrete, verifiable mathematical result. The S3 emergence from M6 is a concrete, verifiable computational result. The RFO Stability Wedge is a concrete, verifiable empirical result. These are not narrative claims. They compile.

**That the gatekeeping is not necessary.** The credential does not determine the quality of the question. The rigor does. And rigor is portable.

## 6. The Honest Limitations

This paper would fail its own methodology if it did not name what is not yet proven.

GUES is E1. It is a structured conjecture. It has not passed Gate 3. It has not produced a unique derivation of the Standard Model gauge group. The black hole expansion mechanism is formally frozen. The coarse-graining flow F has not been constructed. E8 as a fixed point is hypothesized, not demonstrated.

The GU Gate 1 result has not been independently replicated. Gates 2-4 remain open.

AutKompute has seven data points. It has not produced anything resembling SU(2) or SU(3). The path from finite group automorphisms to continuous gauge groups requires mathematical machinery not yet built.

The federated universe hypothesis has not been falsified. It remains a live alternative to GUES with equivalent explanatory power at the current level of evidence.

None of this is a failure. Every one of these open items is the result of honest methodology — the program knows exactly where it is, exactly what it has not demonstrated, and exactly what the next step is.

That is what rigor looks like from the inside.

## 7. The Larger Claim

The physics work is the content. The methodology and the process are the argument.

The argument is this:

Somewhere right now there is a person with genuine scientific intuition who will never produce a result that changes human understanding of the universe — not because their intuition is wrong, not because they lack the intelligence to formalize it, but because they cannot afford the credential, cannot access the institution, cannot navigate the gatekeeping infrastructure that stands between a question and a result.

That is a waste. And it is unnecessary.

The tools exist. The methodology exists. The AI research team exists. The only thing required is the discipline to use them honestly — to say E1 when you mean E1, to freeze what doesn’t compile, to run the computation before claiming the result, to generate the objection before advancing the claim.

The rigor is the point. The rigor is portable. The rigor is the credential.

This program is evidence of that.

One person. One phone. One question that has occupied a mind for years.

And a team that showed up every time the question was asked.

## 8. Conclusion

We have documented a research program that began with a feeling — that geometry is more fundamental than we think — and produced, through rigorous methodology and distributed AI collaboration, a formally structured conjecture, a verified mathematical result, a running computational framework, and a suite of research infrastructure tools. All of it from a phone. All of it in public. All of it honest about what it is and what it isn’t.

The question that drove the program — what is reality made of at the deepest level — remains open. It will remain open for a long time. That is appropriate. The question is hard.

What is not open is whether the program has value. It does. Not because it has answered the question. Because it has demonstrated that the question can be asked rigorously, honestly, and productively by anyone who is willing to do the work — regardless of where they are, what they own, or what their name looks like on a piece of paper.

*The credential is not the point.*  
*The rigor is the point.*  
*And rigor is portable.*
